Tuina (pronounced "twee-nah") is a form of Chinese therapeutic massage that is one of the fundamental treatment methods in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM). Tuina involves the use of various massage techniques to stimulate acupoints, meridians, and muscles, aiming to balance the body's Qi (energy) and promote natural healing. Unlike Western massage, which primarily focuses on relaxation, Tuina is a more dynamic and therapeutic approach that addresses specific health issues. Tuina is based on the same principles as acupuncture, focusing on the flow of Qi through the body's meridians. Practitioners use their hands, fingers, elbows, and sometimes even knees to apply pressure, knead, roll, and rub the body, targeting specific acupoints and areas of tension. The techniques used in Tuina can vary from gentle and soothing to deep and invigorating, depending on the condition being treated. Tuina works by manipulating the soft tissues, muscles, and joints, helping to remove blockages and restore the smooth flow of Qi and blood.
